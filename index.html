Chicana Liberation

The idea of “bridging activism” stands out as the book’s most innovative contribution showing how Chicanas navigated and connected multiple movements, leaving a lasting legacy of intersectional political engagement.

Chávez’s Chicana Liberation recovers the overlooked voices of Chicana activists in Los Angeles, showing how they built a unique “bridging activism” that navigated between the Chicano movement, feminism, and broader social justice struggles.

Introduction

The book is based on Post–World War II to early 1980s Los Angeles. It focuses on recovering the voices of Chicana activists historically overshadowed by male-dominated narratives.

Approach and Methodology

The book uses intersectional lens of race, gender, and class. It also provides a way to humanize history by allowing women to tell their stories.

Intersectionality and Bridging Activism

The book focuses on briding activism. As this concept helped with navigating Chicano, feminist, and social justice movements. It also helped bridged the gaps in the mainstream feminism’s understanding of women of color.
