Amazon.com

Amazon.com is a global e-commerce that allows users to browse, purhcase, and review millions of products across many of categories like electronics, books, clothing, home goods, and more.

The site also features a search-driven interface with personalized recommendations, daily deals, customer reviews, and fast checkout options.

Amazon also supports services like Prime Video, Amazon Fresh, and Kindle. Where this website was designed to provide convenience and efficiency for customers.

Amazon Website

CRAP Analysis

Contrast

Amazon effectively uses contrast to separate the different types of content. For example:

  • Product titles are bold and dark aganist a white background, drawing attention and enhancing readability.
  • Call to Action buttons like "Add to Cart" or "Buy Now" which are typically yellow or orange, standing out from the surrounding elements.

However, Amazon does have some sections during promotions or events like Prime Day can become visually cluttered with many bright colors competing for attention, which may hinder quick navigation.

Repetition

The website maintains consistent layout patterns, button shapes, fonts, and color schemes throughout the site. This repetition helps users learn the interface quickly.

  • The navigation bar at the top stays the same across the pages, with repeated categories like "Today's Deals," "Customer Service," and "Returns & Orders."

Repeating the product layout style like image, title, rating, price also supports easy comparsion and quick decisions for consumers.

Alignment

Amazon's alignment is mostly grid-based and structured, especially in product listings and navigation menus.

  • Text is generally left-aligned, making it easy to read.
  • Filters on the left of the page and product results on the right that creates a clear layout for browsing.

However, with Amazon having promtional banners or personalized ads this can slightly be misaligned or asymmetrcial, which may disrupt the user's reading flow and clarity.

Proximity

Amazon generally groups items logically like prices, product images, and rates are close together, making it easy to scan and digest information.

They also have filtering tools that are clustered in the left-hand panel, which helps users with navigation on the website.

However, the homepage modules can be stacked too tightly like deals, recommended products. This can create a "visual overload" effect that may overhelm new users trying to locate specific content.
